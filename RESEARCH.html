My academic work focuses on multi-agent systems and normative frameworks, exploring how autonomous agents coordinate through norms and social structures in complex, distributed environments.

MULTI-AGENT SYSTEMS CARTOGRAPHY GAMA
November 2024

Automatic Generation of Thematic Maps Using Multi-Agent Systems

I. H. Maminiaina, H. Rakotonirainy, J. Dinaharisoa, T. Ramarozaka, A. Razafinimaro • GAMA Days 2024

This paper presents an approach to automating the generation of thematic maps through multi-agent systems. Agents collaborate to process, interpret, and spatially organize geographic data, reducing the manual effort typically required in cartographic workflows.

MULTI-AGENT SYSTEMS NORMS PHD THESIS
January 2024

Prise en compte des normes dans les comportements des agents

T. Ramarozaka • Université de Fianarantsoa — Thèse de doctorat en Informatique

Cette thèse de doctorat explore comment les normes sociales et organisationnelles peuvent être intégrées dans les comportements des agents autonomes. Elle propose un cadre formel permettant aux agents de percevoir et interpréter les normes.

MULTI-AGENT SYSTEMS NORMS PLANNING
September 2022

Extending Partial-Order Planning to Account for Norms in Agent Behavior

T. Ramarozaka, J. P. Müller, H. L. Rakotonirainy · Conference of the European Social Simulation Association, Springer Nature Switzerland

This paper proposes an extension of partial-order planning to integrate normative constraints into agent behavior. By incorporating norms directly into the planning process, agents can reason about socially acceptable action sequences while still achieving their goals. The approach is evaluated in the context of multi-agent simulations and discussed within the European Social Simulation community.
